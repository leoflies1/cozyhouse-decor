## 3. A Front-Facing Bookshelf — Because Kids Judge Books by Their Covers

Here's what nobody tells you about kids' bookshelves: if the books face backward (spine out), kids don't touch them. If the books face forward (cover out), they grab them constantly. It's that simple.

The [Amdermi 3-Tier Montessori Bookshelf](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0GKXJLRL4?tag=cozyhouse0a-21) is wooden, front-facing, and low enough for toddlers to reach. Three tiers hold 15-20 books depending on size. Natural wood finish matches any nursery style.

Why Montessori: the front-facing design lets kids see the covers and choose for themselves. It builds independence and actually encourages reading. Parents report their kids pulling books off this shelf unprompted — that's the win.

Place it against a wall near the reading corner or next to the crib. Fill it with board books on the bottom tier (for babies) and picture books on top (for toddlers).

## 4. A Soft Round Rug — Because Babies Live on the Floor

Hardwood and tile look beautiful in a nursery. But your baby spends 80% of their time on the floor. That's not a coincidence — they need the space to roll, crawl, and eventually walk. The floor is their whole world.

The [Poowe Cute Animals Round Rug](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0DXPPHZKB?tag=cozyhouse0a-21) is 31.5 inches, washable, non-slip, and has an adorable animal print that works for both boys and girls. Soft enough for tummy time, durable enough for toddler play.

Why round specifically: circular rugs define a play zone without blocking the room's layout. They create a visual "island" that signals "this is where the fun happens." Square rugs eat up corners and make small nurseries feel cramped.

Size tip: 31.5" is perfect for a newborn play mat. If your kid is already walking, go 47-60" for more coverage.
